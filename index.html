Trends in School Enrollment in Kenya

The Kenyan government compiles and opens an impressive amount of data. (Though now behind a login, the data is available here.) When looking at school enrollment by gender across all counties, from grades one through eight, we notice a few trends right off the bat:

  • There are fewer children enrolled in the most rural (eastern) counties
  • Across all counties, enrollment drops consistently from grades 1 to 8 to
  • There are almost equal numbers of girls and boys enrolled across the entire system

Progress Made

The Global Partnership for Education reports that, Kenya's schools must overcome three major hurdles:

  • Gender equality
  • Access to textbooks
  • Sanitation

Challenges Remain

According to UNICEF, Kenya's schools have made huge progress in the last couple decards, particularly in:

  • High student-to-teacher ratio
  • Lack of training for teachers
  • Lack of access to technology in rural settings
